Is there equitable access to food access across the United States? How does food insecurity compare across the United States? Is there equitable access to food access across the United States? The goal of our visualization is to highlight the correlations between national food insecurity and food deserts and various factors, including race and ethnicity, age, access to a vehicle, and geography.

To answer our questions, we used a dataset from the United States Department of Agriculture within their Food Access Research Atlas. We used their most recent dataset from 2015, and wrangled and cleaned the data to remove erroneous data and exclusively focus on the columns we wanted to use for data analysis.

Although we designed our visualizations to be accessible and clear for any audience, our specific target audience are people living in the United States. It is our responsibility to ensure that residents are informed of the food insecurity in their local communities and nationwide, how these problems are exacerbated by race and class, and what individuals can do to contribute to the reduction of this problem.
